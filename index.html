Profile

Who I am, before the models and metrics.

I grew up obsessed with the human body and the systems around it—from atlases of anatomy to hospital hallways, city buses, and algorithmic feeds. My work now lives where behavioral science, research, and technology overlap.

Occupational therapy in India taught me to see how independence and dignity depend on the smallest environmental details. A move to the U.S. for a Master's in Applied Behavior Analysis gave me tools to treat behavior as data about systems, not just people. Writing—poetry first, then essays and policy briefs—has always been how I test and translate ideas.

Today I work on sustainability behavior, digital safety for kids, and responsible AI. I'm most interested in strategies that change what people do without demanding more willpower: better defaults, gentler friction, clearer feedback.
