Who I Am

I'm a behavioral scientist trained in applied behavior analysis and occupational science, currently working at the intersection of research, policy, and technology.

My work is grounded in a simple conviction: behavioral interventions fail when they target individuals instead of systems. Whether I'm studying waste disposal on a university campus or auditing AI outputs for bias, I focus on environmental design, stakeholder alignment, and iterative feedback loops that make the right choice the easy choice.

I've collaborated with academic institutions, public schools, healthcare systems, and government stakeholders. I use mixed-methods research, SQL-based analysis, and behavioral economics to identify root causes, test interventions, and translate findings into operational strategy.

I care about work that is rigorous, equitable, and implementable. I'm not interested in research that lives in journals. I'm interested in research that changes outcomes.

Selected Work

Campus Sustainability Behavior Change

Mixed Methods Policy Design Behavior Change

Context: Led mixed-methods research to improve recycling accuracy on a university campus pursuing zero-waste goals.

Approach: Conducted contextual inquiries, designed a multi-component intervention (signage, education, restructured bin placement), and used single-subject experimental design to measure impact.

Outcome: Achieved a 17.4% improvement in recycling accuracy; findings directly informed campus-wide sustainability policy.

Methods: Qualitative interviews, observational data, SPSS analysis, stakeholder reporting

AI System Performance Diagnostics

AI Auditing Bias Detection Systems Analysis

Context: Adapted the Performance Diagnostic Checklist (PDC)—a tool from organizational behavior analysis—to audit large language model (LLM) outputs for bias and systemic failure modes.

Approach: Systematically tested LLM responses across demographic and contextual variables to identify patterns of inconsistency and bias.

Outcome: Developed a replicable framework for responsible AI auditing that bridges behavioral science and machine learning governance.

Methods: Behavioral diagnostics, bias evaluation, root cause analysis

Digital Safety for Minors

Policy Analysis Child Safety Risk Assessment

Context: Authored a strategic policy brief analyzing the behavioral risks of highly saturated animated content for children on digital platforms.

Approach: Applied developmental psychology and behavioral economics to assess risks; synthesized evidence into actionable policy recommendations.

Outcome: Informed platform safety protocol design and stakeholder education on child-centered content moderation.

Methods: Literature synthesis, risk assessment, policy analysis
